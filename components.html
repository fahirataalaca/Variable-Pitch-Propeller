Key Components

The following are the primary components used in our variable pitch propeller project:

    Stepper Motor (NEMA 17 HS4401) - The primary actuator for the propeller's pitch adjustment.

    DRV8825 Driver - Microstepping driver used to control the stepper motor with microsteps.

    Li-ion Batteries - Compact and rechargeable power source for the motor and electronics.

    Arduino Uno - The microcontroller responsible for controlling the stepper motor.

    3D-Printed Propeller - The propeller blades and housing designed using Fusion 360 and printed with PETG for durability.

    Brushless DC Motor - The motor responsible for spinning the propeller as a whole

    Bearings - The bearings inside the propeller (608 ZZ) are curcial for smooth operations. The pillow stop bearings take the load off the brushless DC motor and ensures that the propeller can spin smooth. Laslty, linear motion bearings are used for the propeller contraption to move on them.

Additional Components

In addition to the core components, the project also includes wiring, and a breadboard for the mechanisms to function.
